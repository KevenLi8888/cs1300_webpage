1 - Identifying Usability Problems

Picking a Web page

https://www.icbc-us.com/ICBC/海外分行/工银美国网站/EN/ProductsServices/PersonalBanking/Deposit/CheckingAccount/checkingaccount.htm

Industrial and Commercial Bank of China (ICBC) is a Chinese state-owned commercial bank with an oversea network in more than 40 countries worldwide. It’s the largest bank in the world by assets, with 47.5% more [1] than the largest bank in North America, JP Morgan Chase & Co, in 2021. ICBC was positioned at 1st in The Banker’s Top 1000 World Banks ranking every year since 2012, and it is one of the world’s largest and most profitable companies [2].

The web page I chose is from the ICBC USA website. It’s the U.S. subsidiary of ICBC, offering financial services and solutions to the Chinese community and students in the U.S., as well as companies that have business relationships with China.

Despite the massive scale of the company, ICBC has a poorly designed website that makes it hard for users to locate the information they need, as well as grasping the most important details of a product.

[1] https://www.thebankerdatabase.com/index.cfm/search/featured

[2] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_and_Commercial_Bank_of_China

Finding Problems

You can clearly tell from the screenshot that this is a poorly designed page.

There are different hierarchies present on the page with different classification methods. Some of the information pages can be reached from multiple entries, while others are hidden deeply and nowhere to be found. Users might find it hard to locate the information they need.

Besides, the information is presented in crowded text blocks, which makes it difficult for users to grasp and the essentials.

The webpage layout is also not flexible and responsive, which makes it unsuitable for the current, mobile-first era.

Accessibility

Using the WAVE Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool, I found many redundant links, low-contrast links and buttons, as well as empty alternative texts, which make it unfriendly for accessibility tools like screenreaders.

2 - Visual Redesign

Low-Fidelity Wireframing

  • I redesigned the navigation bar to make it easier to find the information needed.
  • The most frequently visited pages are personal banking stuffs and their links are shown on the top of the page.
  • The less frequently used but still containing important information are relocated to the footer of the page.
  • I also adopted cards to show different account details. They are clearly organized with a uniformed style and bullet points to highlight the information.

3 - Responsive Redesign

I used Bootstrap for UI components and responsive layout support (containers, grid, columns). All components and elements uses relative attributes for better responsive layout on arbitrary screen sizes.
